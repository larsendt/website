Hi, my name is Dane Larsen. I'm a first year Master's student studying Computer Science at the University of Colorado (at) Boulder. I've been seriously programming for about six years now, with some brief experimentation before that with BASIC on a ZX81 that my Dad helped me build from a kit.

I'm good with C/C++, Python, Qt4, OpenGL/GLSL, OpenCL, Linux (Ubuntu primarily), Vim, Git, and lots of other odds and ends.

I am currently employed at the Laboratory for Atmospheric and Space Physics as a student programmer. I've worked on the new OASIS-CC front-end software that is used to command a number of satellites, and I am currently working with the Messenger/MASCS/UVVS team on 3D visualization program for the instrument data. My proudest achievement so far has been contributing (in a very small way) to the Mars-bound satellite, MAVEN.

Feel free to poke around! I highly recommend the projects page.
